📰 Latest Argentina News
⚽
Messi Hat-Trick Breaks Records (June 16)

Lionel Messi became the oldest World Cup hat-trick scorer (38y 357d) vs Algeria, tying Miroslav Klose's all-time record of 16 WC goals. He is fit and available for the remaining group stage matches.

💪
Scaloni Confirms Full Squad Available

Coach Scaloni confirmed all 26 players are fit after training. Messi's mild hamstring scare ahead of the tournament was resolved. Emiliano Martínez kept a clean sheet on debut.

🎯
Julián Álvarez Stars in Opener

Atlético Madrid forward Julián Álvarez was lively throughout, contributing to the 3-0 victory. His ankle concern pre-tournament is now fully resolved.

🏆
Title Defence on Track

Argentina aim to become the first back-to-back world champions since Brazil in 1958–1962. This would be Messi's 6th World Cup appearance, a personal record.

📅
Argentina vs Austria — June 22

The crucial MD2 clash against Austria at MetLife Stadium kicks off at 3:30 AM IST on June 23. A win would virtually seal qualification for Argentina.

💡 Argentina Trivia & History
🤝
Argentina won the 2022 World Cup defeating France 3-3 (4-2 on penalties) — widely considered the greatest World Cup Final ever played, with Messi and Mbappé both scoring hat-tricks in extra time.
🎽
The iconic light blue and white 'Albiceleste' strip is directly tied to Argentina's national colours, derived from the national flag — first worn at an international in 1902.
📍
Argentina's Group J games are played in Kansas City, Miami, and once more in Kansas City — all in the USA, where Argentina have a large diaspora and fan base.
